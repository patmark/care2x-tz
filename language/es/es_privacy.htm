CARE 2002 and Cookies

Upon your first visit to CARE 2002, CARE 2002 sends a "cookie" to your computer. A cookie is a piece of data that identifies you as a unique user. CARE 2002 uses cookies to improve the quality of our service and to understand our user base more. CARE 2002 does this by storing user preferences in cookies. CARE 2002 will not disclose its cookies to third parties except as required by a valid legal process such as a search warrant, subpoena, statute, or court order.

Most browsers are initially set up to accept cookies. You can reset your browser to refuse all cookies or to indicate when a cookie is being sent. Be aware, however, that most parts of the CARE 2002 may not function properly if you refuse cookies.

What Information Do We Store In The Cookies?

CARE 2002 does not collect any unique information about you (such as your name, email address, etc.) except when you specifically and knowingly provide such information. Except for one cookie, all other cookies are erased automatically upon closing your browser. CARE 2002 notes and saves the following information:

  • Browser language. CARE 2002 uses your browser language to determine which language to use in displaying all the information.
  • Color setup.
  • A randomly generated alphanumeric string (Session ID). This cookie is used to determine whether the following script module is being started by the correct module.
  • A second randomly generated numeric string (User ID). This is a persistent cookie and will remian on your harddrive after closing your browser. This cookie serves to identify you the next time you visit CARE 2002. Identifying you is important so that your individual setup can be done immediately. If you want, you can erase this cookie. Follow your browser's intructions in doing this. Be aware, however, that the default setup will be used next time you visit CARE 2002.
